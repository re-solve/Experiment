In this part,

[Your tasks] You will have to solve 15 tasks. Each task comprises one test question and two estimation questions. In each task, the first estimation question asks you to estimate your confidence in the correctness of your own answer to the test question. The second estimation question asks you to estimate the average correctness rate of all participants. Additionally, in this part, after every 5 tasks, there is a fatigue measurement question. Note: for each test question, you have at most 2 minutes to finish. Once the time is up, the test question will end automatically, and the answer will be submitted.

[Your earnings] In case you are eligible for the bonus, at the end of the experiment, one of the 15 tasks will be randomly selected by computer and you will be paid according to your performance in this chosen task. This means that every answer is potentially relevant for your payment, so that you should think about each task carefully. Specifically,

  • for the test question: you will receive 10 points if you answer to the test question in the chosen task is correct.
  • for the first estimation question: you will receive up to 10 points for your estimation and the closer your estimation is to the truth, the more points you can get. By "closer to the truth", we mean:
    • the lower the confidence level you state when your answer to the test questions is wrong.
    • the higher the confidence level you state when your answer to the test questions is correct.

To view the detailed formula, please click on the triangle.

The formula to calculate points for the first estimation question:

if the answer to the test question is correct, Points = Confidence Level × 10;

if the answer to the test question is wrong, Points = (1 - Confidence Level) × 10
